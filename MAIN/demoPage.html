BINAURAL BEATS

This acoustic illusion is perceived when two different pure-tone
sine waves are presented to a listener dichotically (one through
each ear), with f2 slightly off 2f1.

As a result, a single tone with frequency |2f1 - f2| and
modulated amplitude is perceived. These beats are called
"binaural" because they are originated in the brain:
in the ears, the regions of the cochlea that are responsive
to the two frequencies are far apart.
Hence, note that in this demonstration earphones are
required in order to correctly perceive the effect.

In the first place, two different tones are individually
presented to each ear. Then the two tones are played
simultaneously, reaching after few seconds the frequency
ratio required to ear the beats (oscillating amplitude).

THE SHEPARD TONE

The Shepard Tone is an auditory illusion of a tone that seems to continually
ascend or descend in pitch, yet which ultimately gets no higher or lower.
Here, the effect is presented in a glissando fashion: a continuous
never-ending rising sound is perceived.

Play with us to learn how in works!
Press R to jump and F to duck.
In the lower box an oscilloscope reveals how the infinite scale is made: the illusion
comes from the superposition of 10 dephased oscillators with rising frequencies.
Each bell shape is an oscillator, they are made by visible rectangles only because
of the choice of the FFT size.

Hitting a red obstacle focuses the oscilloscope on a single oscillator, highlighting its behaviour.

The ultimate effect is given by fading in and out each scale/oscillator once it reaches a certain
frequency lower or upper bound, so that hearing the beginning or end of any given scale
is impossible and a continuous sound is perceived.
Avoid the gray obstacles!
Avoid the red obstacles!

Sound Induced Flash Illusion

You are about to be flashed by black disks. Keep the eyes focused on the target.
How many times does each disk flash?

Explanation




If you said one flash each: you are CORRECT!

Sometimes, some people say they see TWO flashes when they hear TWO beeps.

This auditory illusion is a cross-modal effect, in which what is processed in one sensory modality
affects what is processed or experienced in another. What you visually experience is affected by what you hear.
As seen, although the same number of dots flashes on the screen when one and two beeps are sounded,
when there are two beeps, people frequently report experiencing two flashes.
L. Shams and colleagues hypothesise that the “alteration of the visual percept is due to cross-modal perceptual interactions
as opposed to cognitive, attentional, or other origins” (Shams et al., 2002).

Do you hear Yanny or Laurel?




Laurel Yanny

Explanation




This is an auditory illusion which became popular in 2018, in which a short audio recording of speech can be heard as one of two words.
By pitch shifting the original audio to higher or lower frequencies, the same listener can report different interpretations.
When you listen to it with the pitch brought up 30%, you will likely hear LAUREL.
Your brain has so much stimulus at all times that it uses existing information and precise neurological pathways to focus its attention.
Our brain is unconsciously choosing which frequencies in the recording to pay attention to, so:

If you heard LAUREL, you are CORRECT!

The original recording is saying LAUREL, but with higher frequencies overlaid that create ambiguity.
When the audio clip is slowed to lower frequencies, the word YANNY is heard by more listeners.


ILLUSORY DISCONTINUITY

You are about to hear a 5 seconds tone sound.

Is the sound continuous?

Explanation




When those bursts of noise happened, did the tone seem to stop for the duration of the noise?

A 2011 study indicated that roughly one in four people will mistakenly perceive the tone as having been broken up.
There seem to be detectable neurological differences in the brains of people that hear the tone as broken.
The Illusory Discontinuity is an auditory illusion in which a continuous ongoing sound
becomes inaudible during a brief, non-masking noise.

The illusion is perceived only by some listeners, reflecting individual variation in hearing abilities.
The most susceptible listeners describe their sensations in terms of the sound actually containing a physical gap.
The illusory discontinuity is strongest when the interrupting sound is short (50ms). Longer sounds elicit weaker illusory discontinuity.

STEREO SALESMAN

This is an exercise to test your ability in evaluating a good or a bad audio setup.

We will give you 2 samples of the same track played from two very different stereo setups, and you are asked to evaluate how much the sound is really influenced by the quality of the setup you choose.

Do you think the difference of sound quality justifies the difference in price?
Think about it while you listen to the 2 samples!

EXPLANATION

If you thought for a second that the second setup is worth even a cent more than the first one you were tricked as probably many people is everyday: to be honest the audio quality is slightly better in the first sample!

A common trick played by deceitful audio people is to play audio through two systems for the sake of comparison, one after the other, but subtly increase the volume through the system they are trying to promote. This is known as the Stereo Salesman Trick.

This is a very reliable way to convince a naive person that something sounds better, whether it's a stereo or some kind of audio processing plugin, or their mastering services. The naive client in fact won't experience the louder version as louder but just punchier, stronger, or generally more clear.

The louder version may be different than the quieter version, and it might be better or it might be worse, but the loudness will override whatever fair comparison may have happened, in favour of the louder sample. The only way to fairly compare two audio samples is to do it at the same overall volume.

EXPLANATION

Discriminating a particular voice from a crowd of voices is something our auditory processing is pretty good at. This is due to our
ear specialization in listening to human voice: we have a lot more sensitivity in the frequency range of a normal conversation and this allows us to be able to clearly distinguish a voice in a crowd.

Most people have a much easier time hearing what they want once they are keyed into a particular voice, and so a particular frequency range.
