Binaural Beats

This acoustic illusion is perceived when two different pure-tone
sine waves are presented to a listener dichotically (one through
each ear), with f2 slightly off 2f1.

As a result, a single tone with frequency |2f1 - f2| and
modulated amplitude is perceived. These beats are called
"binaural" because they are originated in the brain:
in the ears, the regions of the cochlea that are responsive
to the two frequencies are far apart.
Hence, note that in this demonstration earphones are
required in order to correctly perceive the effect.

In the first place, two different tones are individually
presented to each ear. Then the two tones are played
simultaneously, reaching after few seconds the frequency
ratio required to ear the beats (oscillating amplitude).
